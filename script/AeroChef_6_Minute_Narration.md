# AeroChef — 6-Minute Demo Narration

**Video runtime:** 6:25  
**Narration target:** approximately 6:15–6:22, with brief natural pauses  
**Estimated word count:** approximately 820 words

## 0:00–0:25 — Operations Control Dashboard

Welcome to AeroChef Studio, an AI-enabled platform built for the complexity of inflight culinary operations. The operations control dashboard gives teams a live view of active recipes, approval queues, audit readiness, quality exceptions, upcoming flight trials, and average meal cost. Instead of managing these decisions across spreadsheets, emails, and disconnected systems, AeroChef brings the entire culinary lifecycle into one governed workspace.

## 0:25–0:43 — Recipe Library

The Recipe Library becomes the single source of truth for every approved, draft, and in-development meal. Culinary teams can search by recipe, ingredient, airline, station, cabin class, or status, then open a standardized recipe card with the operational and commercial context needed for a quick decision.

## 0:43–1:28 — Structured Recipe Design

When a new airline recipe is created, AeroChef guides the developer through a structured specification rather than a free-form document. Ingredients are selected from approved masters, with quantities, units, preparation notes, and allergen information captured consistently. The process and equipment stage records industrial preparation steps, cooking methods, chilling requirements, holding controls, and equipment dependencies. Yield and waste trials then capture the mass balance from raw input through preparation, cooking, blast chilling, portioning, and final packed weight. This creates accurate cost-per-gram and cost-per-portion visibility. Finally, cabin and altitude profiles connect the recipe to the environment in which the passenger will experience it.

## 1:28–1:42 — Reusable Recipe Standards

Back in the library, teams can compare recipes, review their readiness, and reuse approved specifications. Innovation becomes faster without losing standardization, traceability, or control.

## 1:42–2:06 — Ingredient Master Catalog

The Ingredient Master Catalog controls the foundation of recipe quality. Each ingredient is linked to its category, approved supplier, purchase unit, cost, allergen status, and availability. Governed ingredient masters prevent unapproved substitutions and allow any supplier price or specification change to flow into recipe costing and production planning.

## 2:06–2:28 — Meal Tray Engineering

Meal Tray Engineering moves beyond the recipe to the complete passenger service unit. Teams select an aircraft and cabin profile, define the tray configuration, and assemble the main course, sides, accompaniments, and service components. AeroChef calculates engineered tray weight, estimated tray cost, and operational fit, helping teams balance passenger experience with loading, handling, and galley constraints.

## 2:28–2:56 — Production Scaling

Once a design is approved, Production Scaling converts one validated recipe into an executable batch plan. The operator selects the recipe and required portion volume. AeroChef applies actual yield factors to calculate raw ingredient quantities, process quantities, batch weight, container requirements, and estimated production cost. When moving from a trial batch to hundreds or thousands of portions, purchasing and kitchen teams receive numbers based on measured yield rather than simple multiplication.

## 2:56–3:18 — Reviews and Approvals

The Reviews and Approvals workspace provides controlled sign-off. Culinary, food safety, nutrition, commercial, and operations reviewers can inspect evidence, complete checks, request revisions, or approve the specification. Conflict controls prevent inappropriate self-approval, while the audit trail preserves who changed what, when, and why.

## 3:18–3:48 — Global Logistics Master Data

AeroChef also maintains the global logistics masters behind every meal. Airline records define commercial and service contexts. Catering station profiles capture local capabilities, lead times, currencies, markups, and operating constraints. Supplier masters connect ingredients to approved sources and terms. Most importantly, cabin profiles model aircraft type, equivalent altitude, humidity, ambient noise, cabin class, and flight duration, allowing the same culinary design to be evaluated differently for different aircraft and routes.

## 3:48–4:23 — Aero-Culinary Copilot

The Aero-Culinary Copilot gives users a conversational way to interrogate this governed data. A developer can ask for recipe risks, production implications, or recommended adjustments, and the assistant responds using the selected recipe and operational context. It does not replace the chef or approver. It accelerates analysis, surfaces the factors behind a recommendation, and makes the reasoning visible for human review.

## 4:23–4:57 — Sensory Analysis

In Sensory Analysis, the user selects a recipe and asks the platform to evaluate how sweetness, saltiness, acidity, aroma, texture, moisture, and spice perception may shift in the cabin. AeroChef combines the recipe specification with the chosen aircraft profile to identify likely sensory drift. The result highlights what may become muted, what may become harsh, and which attributes need protection during reheating and service.

## 4:57–5:28 — Altitude Reformulation

Altitude Reformulation turns that analysis into a controlled engineering recommendation. The platform can propose mathematically adjusted salt, moisture, sauce, acid, aroma, and texture parameters for the selected aircraft environment. Recommendations remain linked to the original recipe, include the rationale behind every change, and can be routed through trials and approvals before becoming a production standard.

## 5:28–6:25 — Grounded Recommendation and Conclusion

The final demonstration shows how the Copilot answers a practical high-altitude question in seconds. It summarizes the effect of lower pressure and dry cabin air, identifies the recipe elements most exposed to flavor loss, and proposes targeted actions rather than a generic seasoning increase. Because every recommendation is grounded in recipe, yield, supplier, station, and cabin data, the output is actionable for chefs, engineers, operations, and commercial teams. AeroChef transforms inflight catering from a fragmented sequence of handoffs into a precise, scalable, and continuously improving operating system. It protects culinary intent, improves cost and yield control, reduces waste, strengthens compliance, and helps deliver a consistent passenger experience from the test kitchen to thirty-five thousand feet.
